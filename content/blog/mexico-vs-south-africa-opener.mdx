The 2026 World Cup begins where the 1970 and 1986 tournaments began —
the Estadio Azteca, 2,240 metres above sea level. Mexico open as the
host nation favourite against a South Africa side that came through a
brutal CAF qualifying group on goal difference.

## The altitude question

The Azteca's altitude has historically been the single biggest factor in
opening fixtures held there. Visiting teams' high-press metrics typically
drop 12-18% versus their qualifying baselines, especially in the second
half. South Africa played zero qualifiers above 1,500m. Mexico played
six home matches at 2,240m in the eighteen months leading up to the
tournament.

## What the model likes

Mexico are short across all five Serbian books — Mozzart open at 1.65 on

not conceded more than two goals in any of their last eleven matches.

## What to watch

- Mexico's pressing intensity in the opening 20 minutes — set the tone
  for whether this becomes a 1-0 or 3-1 game.
- South Africa's set-piece delivery. They scored five of their last
  eight goals from dead-ball situations.
- The referee. CONCACAF's appointed crew tends to let more contact go
  than UEFA officials, which favours the more physical side (RSA).

Kickoff is 19:00 local (CDMX), 02:00 the next morning in Belgrade.
